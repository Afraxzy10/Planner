Risk Warnings
🔴 Risk 1: NPTEL content overload
12 weeks × 2 courses = 72 hrs of video. You have 9 days. You MUST watch at 1.5–2x speed and take only bullet notes — not detailed notes. If you try to watch at 1x and take full notes, you will run out of time. Speed is the strategy here.
🔴 Risk 2: 6th Sem — never read materials, exams by May 6
Starting from zero with 5 subjects and exams by May 6 is CRITICAL pressure. You have roughly 2 weeks after NPTEL (Apr 21–May 5). You must start Chapter 1 of Subject 1 on April 21 without a single day of delay. Every subject needs at least 2–3 days of focused reading. Start the moment NPTEL exams are done.
🟡 Risk 3: Trying to do everything daily
You cannot give 100% to 7 goals simultaneously. This plan uses phase locking — in Phase 1, NPTEL is 80% of energy. DSA gets 20% (maintenance). Trying to balance equally = failing at everything.
🟡 Risk 4: IELTS — zero start, Band 7 target
Band 7 from zero needs minimum 3–4 months of consistent daily practice. If you start in May and register in July, that's tight but possible IF you practice writing + reading daily. Vocabulary is the fastest lever — start 20 words/day from Day 1.
🟢 Strength: Python basics known
This is your biggest advantage for DSA + placements. You're not starting from zero. Build on this immediately — transition from basic Python to DSA problems using Python from Day 1.
