Resume Analysis & 100+ Interview Questions

⚠️ Every claim on your resume is a potential interview question. Know every number, dataset, and decision you made.

Resume Strengths

Strong Claims

  • 97.3% ensemble accuracy — specific and verifiable
  • Drebin-215 dataset — real, well-known benchmark
  • ~17,500 samples, ~215K features — data credibility
  • Individual model stats (95.2%, 96.1%, 94.7%)
  • IBM Hackathon finals — 10,000+ participants
  • CGPA 8.6, top 5% in Statistics

Potential Weak Points

  • "Production-grade" — strong claim for a student
  • Streamlit inference interface — depth will be probed
  • 30% client increase at Technosprint — unverifiable
  • CNN on tabular/binary data — unusual choice
  • No dataset link, Kaggle profile, or GitHub shown

Likely Challenge Areas

CNN on binary features: Interviewers will ask why you used CNN on tabular binary data. Know your answer: you reshaped features into 2D matrix form and treated them as image-like structures, OR you treated feature groups as "channels." Be honest if you were experimenting.

LSTM on static features: LSTM is for sequential data. On Drebin binary features, this is unconventional. Be ready to explain: sequential treatment of API call sequences, or honest framing as an architectural experiment.

"Production-grade": This implies deployment, scaling, CI/CD. Either remove this or be ready to defend it precisely (Streamlit app counts, but clarify it's a demo-grade system).
